Which insects native to Norway are the best feed for chickens?

In Norway, as in many other parts of the world, chickens can be fed a variety of insects for added protein and nutrition. While the specific insects available may vary depending on the region and season, here are some common insects that are native to Norway and can be suitable as chicken feed:

  • Mealworms: Mealworms are widely available and are a great source of protein for chickens. They can be purchased from pet stores or raised at home.
  • Crickets: Crickets are another excellent source of protein and are commonly available as chicken feed. They can also be raised at home, although this may require some effort.

Which of these best replace supplements like soy and rapeseed oil?

When it comes to replacing supplements like soy and rapeseed oil in a chicken's diet, you'll want to focus on providing alternative sources of essential nutrients such as protein and fat. Here are some options:

Protein Sources:

  • Insects: As mentioned earlier, insects like mealworms, crickets, and black soldier fly larvae are rich in protein and can serve as excellent alternatives to soy in a chicken's diet. They are also closer to the natural diet of chickens.
  • Fish Meal: Fish meal is a high-protein supplement made from ground fish. It's a good source of essential amino acids and can replace some of the protein provided by soy.
  • Peas and Lentils: These legumes are relatively high in protein and can be included in the chicken's diet as a source of plant-based protein.
  • Sunflower Seeds: Sunflower seeds are a source of both protein and fat. They can be added to the diet in moderation to provide essential nutrients.

Fat Sources:

  • Insects: Insects such as black soldier fly larvae are not only high in protein but also contain a good amount of fat. They can partially replace the fat provided by rapeseed oil.
  • Flaxseeds: Flaxseeds are a source of healthy fats, particularly omega-3 fatty acids. You can incorporate ground flaxseeds into the chicken's diet as a source of essential fats.
  • Sunflower Seeds: Sunflower seeds mentioned earlier also contain fats, which can partially replace the fat from rapeseed oil.
  • Fish Oil: If you are looking for a direct replacement for the omega-3 fatty acids found in rapeseed oil, you can consider adding fish oil to the chicken's diet in controlled amounts. Fish oil is rich in omega-3s.

It's important to note that a balanced diet for chickens should include a variety of ingredients to ensure they receive all the necessary nutrients. When making dietary changes, do so gradually and consult with a poultry nutritionist or veterinarian to ensure that your chickens' nutritional needs are met. The specific replacements you choose will depend on the availability of these ingredients in your region and the dietary requirements of your chickens.
